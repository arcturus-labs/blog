## Description

What does it actually look like to run a dark factory in production — a software development pipeline where AI agents write code, review pull requests, and ship features with minimal human oversight?

Dave Lane (CEO) and Jami Couch (CTO) of Twin Sun are early practitioners. In this conversation we get into the real lessons from building their factory, called Scarif. The biggest one: you can't design the whole system and flip it on. You introduce one step at a time, run it manually until you trust it, and only then let it go dark. And the insight that made it all click: stop trying to build a general-purpose factory. Build *your* factory — constrain the agent to the patterns your company already uses, and the output becomes dramatically more dependable.

We also cover how their AI PR reviewer went from optional advice to autonomously approving 70% of pull requests, why visual design review is the hardest problem they're still working through, how to get a skeptical dev team on board, and how the factory pattern might apply to business processes well beyond code.
