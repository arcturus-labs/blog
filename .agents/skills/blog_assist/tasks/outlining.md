# Outlining

John often writes the dense outline himself — it's a personal thinking tool and he's good at it. Don't assume he wants help unless he asks.

## What John's outlines look like

The outline is typically very dense: bullets that are nearly sentence-length, with nested sub-points that already carry the substance of the argument. It reads almost like collapsed prose. This is intentional.

## If John asks for help

Help turn ideas into structure:
- Suggest section order — what earns the opening slot? what's the payoff that needs to come last?
- Identify the **vehicle** (the narrative frame: timeline, problem–solution, analogy, contrast, etc.) if it isn't settled yet
- Point out gaps: is there a claim that needs support? a transition that's missing?
- Flag anything that seems like it belongs in a different post

Don't rewrite his bullets into prose yet — that's the next phase. Preserve his language and specificity.

## Where to save

If the outline is being built from scratch or iterated significantly with your help, save it alongside the brainstorm notes:

```
work-in-progress/<post-slug>/outline.md
```
