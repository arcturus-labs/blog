# The Workflow Engineer

**Core idea:** Deep domain knowledge — the kind that isn't written down anywhere — is AI's current hard limit. The emerging role that bridges this gap is someone who understands both agents and the domain they're being deployed into.

---

## Quote Sequence (~45 sec)

**[00:28:25]**
> "The vast majority of knowledge about Nitinol is not written down. AI is not going to touch that for a while."

**[00:28:42]**
> "You throw an agent into the mix and eventually they start picking up things and they'll be more and more helpful. But ultimately, you still need someone who knows the domain to even get an agent in the door and configure it so that it starts picking up the knowledge and becoming more useful."

**[00:29:01]**
> "I've been floating this job title of a workflow engineer — someone who is able to think about agents and how they fit into teams from a systems perspective, but who also has that domain expertise. Like someone who knows about manufacturing processes and also knows how to work with agents, how to work them into workflows."
