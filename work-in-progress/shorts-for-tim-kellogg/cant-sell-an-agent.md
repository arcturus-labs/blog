# You Can't Just Sell an Agent

**Core idea:** Dropping a generic AI agent into a company captures only a fraction of the potential value. Real transformation comes from encoding domain knowledge and changing how the organization works — not from the tool itself.

---

## Quote Sequence (~50 sec)

**[00:00:29]**
> "A generic agent only gets you maybe 10% of the potential value. The other 90% comes from encoding your company's bespoke domain knowledge into the agent. Skip that step and you're leaving most of the benefit on the table."

**[00:12:43]**
> "If you give someone a tool, it's just yet another tool. They have to learn how to use it. They have to fit into the workflow."

**[00:12:57]**
> "Fundamentally, the real problem is you need to transform the workflow to take advantage of AI."

**[00:14:35]**
> "Can you sell an agent? You can sell it, I guess. But will it transform a company? Not convinced."

**[00:15:02]**
> "The people that use it really well are going to make good use of it for individual tasks. But it's leaving so much potential on the table. The potential is in understanding not just this activity they did right now, but learning from that activity — and wiring up all these activities together so that over time the business process becomes codified in the agent."
