New video: Twin Sun built a real dark factory. Here's what they learned.

I just published a new video conversation with Dave Lane and Jami Couch — CEO and CTO of Twin Sun, a Nashville software agency that has already built and is running a real dark factory. That's a software development pipeline where AI agents write code, review pull requests, and ship features with minimal human oversight.

The conversation is full of hard-won lessons. The biggest one: you can't design the whole system and flip it on. You introduce one step at a time, run it manually until you trust it, and only then let it go dark. And the insight that made everything click: stop trying to build a general-purpose factory — build your factory. Constrain the agents to the patterns your team already uses, and the output becomes dramatically more dependable.
