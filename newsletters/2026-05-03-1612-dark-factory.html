New post + video: Twin Sun built a real dark factory. Here's what they learned.

I just published a new post and video with Dave Lane and Jami Couch - CEO and CTO of Twin Sun, a Nashville software agency that has built and is running a real dark factory. That's a software development pipeline where AI agents write code, review pull requests, and ship features with minimal human oversight. Their PR review bot currently approves 70% of pull requests on its own. No human sees that code until it's already merged.

Two principles made it work:

  • Build your factory, not a generic factory. Constrain the agents to how your team already writes code. Generic pipelines give the agent too much rope.
  • Go gradually darker. Earn trust before removing the human from each step. Don't flip the whole thing on at once.

The post covers both in detail. The video goes even deeper.
